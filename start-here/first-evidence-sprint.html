First Evidence Sprint

The first real fieldwork — turning discovery interviews into structured evidence

Start Here › First Evidence Sprint

This page runs the first evidence sprint. It is the working interface for collecting real discovery evidence in the audit and professional-services segments.

Planned · v0.1 No interviews logged yet Open the interview script

This is the moment the lab stops running on synthetic data and starts collecting real discovery evidence. It changes no scoring model, no dashboards, and no confidence grades — it only collects evidence. The full plan also lives in the repo at docs/FIRST_EVIDENCE_SPRINT.md; this page is its working, in-browser version.

Sprint at a glance

Goal

First real evidence on the Market Entry and Positioning claims — signal, not verdicts.

Target segments

Audit firms · Professional services firms

Interview target

8–10 total (~5 audit + ~4 professional services)

Minimum evidence threshold

≥ 3 per segment with evidence_quality_score ≥ 40 and bias_risk ≤ 66

Sprint goal. Replace the “no real evidence yet” state on the Decision 1 and Decision 2 claims with the first set of real, structured discovery evidence. Not a goal: validating any claim, moving any claim to grade A, or committing to a segment or positioning.

The fieldwork workflow

  • 1Conduct interview
  • →2Enter raw data
  • →3Map to evidence
  • →4Review claims
  • →5Decide if status moves

    • 1 · How to conduct an interview

    1. Run the call with the discovery interview script (~40 min, 13 sections from respondent context to permission to follow up). Every question maps to a decision, a claim, and a CSV field.
    2. Run the positioning message test inside the call to test the three framings (CRM · Revenue Operations · AI Readiness).
    3. Score the interview the same day with the scoring guide — anchored 0–100 rubrics for every signal, plus quality and bias.

    Interviewer discipline. Do not lead the witness; ask open questions first. Record what was actually said (verbatim in notes). One interview is a single data point — it never validates a claim. Leave a field blank if it was not covered; do not fabricate it.

    2 · How to enter raw interview data

    Add one row per interview to data/raw/discovery_interviews.csv, filling every covered field:

    interview_id, date, company_segment, company_size, respondent_role, decision_linked, claim_linked,
pain_score, budget_signal, urgency_signal, current_tooling, crm_maturity, workflow_maturity,
ai_readiness_maturity, positioning_preference, willingness_to_pay_signal, sales_cycle_signal, notes,
evidence_quality_score, bias_risk, supports_or_refutes

    All signal scores are 0–100 (see the scoring guide). Set decision_linked, claim_linked, and supports_or_refutes (supports / refutes / mixed / neutral) for the primary claim.

    3 · How to map interview data into processed evidence

    Using the evidence-capture step, add one row per claim the interview bears on to data/processed/discovery_evidence.csv:

    evidence_id, interview_id, decision_id, claim_id, source_type, source_name, date_collected,
quality_score, bias_risk, supports_or_refutes, evidence_summary, confidence_impact, notes

    Apply the conversions from the scoring guide:

    Raw interview field (0–100) Processed field Conversion
    evidence_quality_score quality_score ÷ 100 → 0.0–1.0
    bias_risk bias_risk low (0–33) · medium (34–66) · high (67–100)
    supports_or_refutes supports_or_refutes copy unchanged

    Set source_type = interview and confidence_impact = raises / lowers / none (direction only — never a grade). The Evidence Register is where these records land.

    4 · How to run pytest and quarto render

    source .venv/bin/activate
pytest            # validators check columns, grades (A–D), supports/refutes, and score ranges
quarto render     # the decision + Claims & Confidence pages recompute from the new records

    Both must pass before you trust anything. A red suite is a hard stop.

    5 · How to review results

    • Claims & Confidence — each tested claim now shows a real evidence count and a supports/refutes split instead of “none yet”.
    • Market Entry and Positioning — the claim cards reflect the new evidence.
    • Current Status — the top-level read of where the decisions stand.
    • Ask, per claim: did independent interviews converge, or does the signal conflict?

    What not to conclude yet

    • Do not declare a first-target segment or a positioning — a handful of interviews is grade-C signal at best.
    • Do not move any claim to grade A (validated); that needs a pilot with measured impact.
    • Do not treat a stated positioning preference as a won deal.
    • Do not let an enthusiastic-but-biased interview carry a claim — that is what bias_risk is for.
    • If a claim’s evidence_needed_for_B bar is genuinely met, change its grade deliberately and record why (a note or an ADR). Grade changes are decisions, not edits.

    This sprint produces evidence, not verdicts. The lab stays at v0.1 hypothesis stage until the evidence earns otherwise.

## Claims under test

The claims below read live from `data/sample/claim_register.csv` (Decisions 1 and 2). Each is a
**hypothesis** — the sprint gathers first signal on it, nothing more.

```{python}
#| label: claims-under-test
#| echo: false
import sys, pathlib
_root = pathlib.Path.cwd()
while _root != _root.parent and not (_root / "src").is_dir():
    _root = _root.parent
sys.path.insert(0, str(_root))
from IPython.display import HTML
from src.registers import load_claim_register, claim_evidence_counts, _grade_chip, _esc

claims = load_claim_register()
under_test = claims[claims["decision_id"].isin(["D1", "D2"])]

rows = []
for _, c in under_test.iterrows():
    counts = claim_evidence_counts(c["claim_id"])
    rows.append(
        "<tr>"
        f'<td><code>{_esc(c["claim_id"])}</code></td>'
        f'<td>{_esc(c["decision_id"])}</td>'
        f'<td>{_esc(c["claim_type"])}</td>'
        f'<td>{_esc(c["claim_text"])}</td>'
        f'<td>{_grade_chip(c["current_confidence_grade"])}</td>'
        f'<td>{counts["total"]}</td>'
        "</tr>"
    )
HTML(
    '<table class="table"><thead><tr>'
    '<th>Claim</th><th>Decision</th><th>Type</th><th>Hypothesis</th><th>Grade</th>'
    '<th>Real evidence</th></tr></thead><tbody>' + "".join(rows) + '</tbody></table>'
)
```

### 2 · How to enter raw interview data
