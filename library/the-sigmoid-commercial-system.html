The Sigmoid Commercial System

An evidence-based framework for building healthier businesses

Version: 1.0 · Status: Foundational document · Owner: [Name] · Last updated: [Date]

This is the root document. Everything Sigmoid builds, writes, researches, or sells inherits from the ideas set out here. Where a product decision, a piece of content, or a strategy disagrees with this document, this document governs the thinking; the artifact is adjusted, not the system.

Preface

Businesses have never had more customer data than they do today. Yet many still struggle to answer simple questions:

  • Why are we losing customers?
  • Why are some salespeople consistently successful and others not?
  • Which customers should we focus on?
  • Why are our forecasts inaccurate?
  • Why does growth feel unpredictable?

Most organisations collect information. Few transform that information into evidence. Fewer still use that evidence to improve their commercial decisions.

The Sigmoid Commercial System (SCS) was developed to address this gap. It is a practical framework for understanding how businesses grow, how commercial performance can be measured, and how better evidence leads to better decisions.

It is not a CRM methodology. It is not a sales methodology. It is a way of understanding commerce itself — and, from that understanding, a way of building healthier businesses.

Part I — The Nature of Commerce

Every business exists to create value for customers.

Whether it sells software, manufactures products, provides professional services, or operates a mine, every organisation faces the same commercial challenge: it must find customers, earn their trust, deliver value, retain their business, and grow those relationships over time.

Success is rarely determined by a single event. It is the result of thousands of commercial decisions made every day:

  • Which customers should we pursue?
  • Which opportunities deserve attention?
  • Which proposal should we submit?
  • Should we discount?
  • Which customers are at risk?
  • Where should we invest next?

Every one of these decisions shapes the future of the business. Businesses often describe the results as luck, experience, or instinct. We see them differently:

Revenue is the outcome of commercial decisions. Customer retention is the outcome of commercial decisions. Growth is the outcome of commercial decisions.

Even failure is usually the accumulated result of commercial decisions that were delayed, uninformed, or simply wrong.

The central question, therefore, is not “how do we make better software?” It is:

How do we help organisations make better commercial decisions?

That question is the reason the SCS exists.

Part II — Businesses Are Commercial Systems

A business is not a collection of departments. It is a living commercial system in which every part influences the others.

  • Marketing influences lead quality.
  • Lead quality influences sales.
  • Sales influence customer experience.
  • Customer experience influences retention.
  • Retention influences growth.
  • Growth influences investment.
  • Investment creates new opportunities.

Customers do not appear by chance. Sales do not happen in isolation. Loyalty is not automatic. Growth is not accidental. Everything is connected — and the connections form a cycle:

        Customers
           ↓
       Relationships
           ↓
        Decisions
           ↓
         Actions
           ↓
        Outcomes
           ↓
        Learning
           ↓
        Customers  (repeat)

Seeing the business as a system is the first shift the SCS asks for. Once you see the system, you stop treating symptoms in one department and start improving the flows between them.

Part III — The Five Commercial Questions

Beneath the system are five questions every business answers continuously, whether it realises it or not:

  1. How do we find customers?
  2. How do we win customers?
  3. How do we keep customers?
  4. How do we grow customers?
  5. How do we make better decisions?

Everything in commerce maps to one of these. They are the load-bearing pillars of the SCS: every capability, every metric, every decision, and every tool exists to help a business answer one of them better. The fifth question sits beneath the other four — because how well you decide determines how well you find, win, keep, and grow.

Part IV — Commercial Health

Financial statements describe what has already happened. They are the accounting of the past.

Commercial Health describes whether the business is likely to succeed tomorrow. It is the leading indicator that financial statements cannot provide.

Commercial Health reflects an organisation’s ability to:

  • Attract the right customers.
  • Convert opportunities into sales.
  • Build strong customer relationships.
  • Retain customers.
  • Grow existing accounts.
  • Predict future revenue.
  • Learn from commercial performance.

Commercial Health is not a single metric. It is a system of dimensions, each observable and each improvable:

Dimension What it reflects
Lead Quality Are we attracting the right customers?
Sales Effectiveness Do we convert the right opportunities?
Relationship Quality Are our customer relationships strong and current?
Customer Retention Do customers stay?
Customer Growth Do relationships grow in value over time?
Revenue Predictability Can we forecast with confidence?
Decision Quality Are our commercial decisions consistently good?

Because these dimensions are observable, Commercial Health can be measured — and what can be measured can be improved. Over time, the SCS expresses Commercial Health as a score, so a business can see its condition, track it, and act on it, the way it already does with financial health.

Commercial Health is the central framework of the SCS. Everything else exists to move it.

Part V — The Commercial Learning Loop

If Commercial Health is what we improve, the Commercial Learning Loop is how we improve it. It is the most important model in the system.

   Evidence
      ↓
   Insight
      ↓
   Decision
      ↓
   Action
      ↓
   Outcome
      ↓
   Learning
      ↓
   Evidence  (repeat)

The loop turns on a distinction most businesses miss:

Information tells us what happened. Evidence helps explain why it happened.

Businesses collect enormous amounts of information — customer records, emails, meetings, sales activities, invoices, support requests — and most of it is never transformed into evidence. That is why businesses do not improve simply because they have more data. They improve because they understand what their data means.

Evidence creates insight; insight improves decisions; decisions drive actions; actions create outcomes; outcomes generate new evidence — and the cycle repeats. Businesses that learn faster improve faster.

Everything Sigmoid builds — CRM, analytics, AI, audits, dashboards, the Commercial Intelligence Lab — exists to make this loop turn faster and more honestly.

Part VI — Commercial Capabilities

Businesses do not improve by intention. They improve by developing capabilities — repeatable strengths that raise Commercial Health. The SCS names eight:

  • Acquire — attract the right customers.
  • Qualify — focus effort on the opportunities that matter.
  • Sell — convert opportunities into customers, consistently.
  • Deliver — keep the promises that were made.
  • Retain — keep customers, and notice early when they are at risk.
  • Grow — expand relationships in value over time.
  • Measure — see commercial performance clearly and honestly.
  • Improve — turn what is measured into better decisions.

Each capability can be assessed, developed, and strengthened. Together they are the practical means by which a business raises its Commercial Health.

Part VII — Commercial Maturity

Not every business operates the loop equally well. Commercial maturity describes how consistently and how evidence-led a business’s commercial decisions are. The SCS defines five levels, so an organisation can locate itself and see the next step.

Level Name How the business decides
1 Reactive Decisions made ad hoc, from memory and instinct.
2 Organised Consistent processes exist, but decisions rest on opinion.
3 Measured Performance is tracked; decisions use metrics.
4 Evidence-Driven Decisions are made from evidence, with explicit confidence.
5 Commercial Intelligence The learning loop is systematic; the business improves by design.

Maturity is not about company size or software spend. It is about how decisions are made. The goal of the SCS is to move an organisation up this ladder.

Part VIII — Decision Frameworks

The SCS becomes practical at the point of decision. The same commercial decisions recur in every business:

  • Which lead do we pursue?
  • Which customer do we prioritise?
  • Which proposal do we submit?
  • Which opportunity do we back?
  • Which investment do we make?

The SCS approach to each is the same: decide with evidence, and be explicit about confidence. A good decision names the question, the evidence behind it, the reasoning used, and how sure we are — so it can be reviewed, learned from, and improved. This is where structured decision frameworks, and in time AI, help: not by making the decision for us, but by making the evidence and the confidence visible. (This is the discipline the Commercial Intelligence Lab operationalises.)

Part IX — The Role of Technology

Technology is where most companies begin. In the SCS, it comes last — on purpose.

CRM is not the system. CRM supports the system.

Analytics supports the system. AI supports the system. Every tool exists to help the business run the Commercial Learning Loop and raise its Commercial Health:

  • CRM captures the relationships and activity that become evidence.
  • Analytics turns that evidence into insight.
  • AI strengthens human judgement at the point of decision — it does not replace it.

Technology enables; it does not replace thinking. A business does not become healthier because it owns more software. It becomes healthier because it consistently makes better commercial decisions — and uses technology to do so.

Part X — The Sigmoid Principles

The system reduces to ten principles. Everything above is their explanation; everything Sigmoid does is their application.

  1. Businesses are commercial systems.
  2. Every commercial outcome is the result of previous decisions.
  3. Better evidence leads to better decisions.
  4. Better decisions improve Commercial Health.
  5. Customer relationships are measurable.
  6. Follow-up is a competitive advantage.
  7. AI should strengthen human judgement.
  8. Revenue is earned through consistent commercial execution.
  9. Learning is a commercial capability.
  10. Evidence over opinion.

What this means — how everything inherits from the SCS

This document is the foundation. Everything Sigmoid produces should trace back to it:

CRM (Sigmoid CRM) implements the system
Commercial Health measures the system

And it gives us one test for everything we build, write, or ship:

Does this help someone make a better commercial decision?

If the answer is yes, it belongs at Sigmoid. If the answer is no, it doesn’t.

Our mission follows directly: to help organisations make better commercial decisions through evidence. Sometimes that means CRM software. Sometimes analytics. Sometimes AI. Sometimes a decision framework. Technology is never the purpose — it is one of the tools. The purpose does not change.
