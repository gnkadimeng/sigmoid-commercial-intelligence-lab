Sigmoid — Commercial Messaging Framework

What we say, repeatedly, until the market associates us with it

Version: 1.0 · Status: Working document · Owner: [Name] · Last updated: [Date] Scope: The commercial voice of Sigmoid — expressed primarily through Sigmoid CRM, consistent with the wider Sigmoid Analytica promise, we make the world transparent. Inherits from: The Sigmoid Commercial System (the foundational document). The SCS is the worldview; this framework turns it into words.

How to use this framework

Our Content Strategy answers why we create content. This framework answers the harder, more important question:

What exactly are we going to say — repeatedly — until the market associates us with it?

It is the bridge between strategy and every post, article, talk, and sales conversation. It is not a campaign; it is a vocabulary and a set of ideas we repeat on purpose until they become ours in the market’s mind.

Read Parts A–C in order:

  • Part A — The Foundation: the one idea we want to own and the architecture beneath it.
  • Part B — The Toolkit: nine reservoirs of raw material for content.
  • Part C — Putting it to work: how to turn the toolkit into content, our elevator pitches, and the rules of consistency.

The golden rule: don’t start with the product. Start with commerce. Every business exists to answer five questions —

  1. How do we find customers?
  2. How do we win customers?
  3. How do we keep customers?
  4. How do we grow customers?
  5. How do we make better decisions?

Everything Sigmoid CRM does fits into one of those. So does everything we say.

Part A — The Foundation

1. The Big Idea we want to own: Commercial Health

Most brands publish random advice and are remembered for nothing. Great brands own one idea. Ours is Commercial Health.

Every business measures its financial health, its operational health, and increasingly its employee health. Almost no one measures their commercial health — yet every business has it, and it determines whether they grow or quietly decline.

Positioning statement. Sigmoid helps businesses measure, understand, and improve their Commercial Health — their ability to find, win, keep, and grow customers.

This is our unifying concept. Instead of publishing scattered CRM tips, we become the company that helps organisations improve their Commercial Health. Every article, post, conversation, and product capability reinforces that single idea — which makes our messaging coherent and easy to remember.

Commercial Health, made measurable

Because we are an evidence-led company, we don’t leave “health” as a metaphor. Commercial Health is defined canonically in The Sigmoid Commercial System as seven observable dimensions — and what can be observed can be improved:

Dimension What it reflects
Lead Quality Are we attracting the right customers?
Sales Effectiveness Do we convert the right opportunities?
Relationship Quality Are our customer relationships strong and current?
Customer Retention Do customers stay?
Customer Growth Do relationships grow in value over time?
Revenue Predictability Can we forecast with confidence?
Decision Quality Are our commercial decisions consistently good?

Together these form a Commercial Health check — a diagnostic a business can run on itself. It is a content engine, a sales conversation starter, and, in time, a product capability. When someone asks “what does Sigmoid do?”, the honest answer is: we make your Commercial Health visible, and help you improve it.

Part B — The Messaging Toolkit

Nine reservoirs. Each one alone is worth hundreds of pieces of content.

3. Our View of the World — Beliefs

Thought leadership begins with beliefs, not products. State them plainly and people immediately understand what Sigmoid stands for.

We believe…

  • Businesses don’t fail because they lack data — they fail because they don’t know what their data is telling them.
  • Businesses don’t need more software. They need better commercial decisions.
  • Customer relationships are a business asset — yet most businesses manage them like an address book.
  • The quality of a business is reflected in the quality of its customer relationships.
  • Revenue growth isn’t luck. It’s the result of thousands of better commercial decisions.
  • AI shouldn’t replace people. It should help them make better decisions.
  • A CRM shouldn’t just record yesterday. It should improve tomorrow.

4. The Commercial Journey — our messaging map

Don’t think in features. Think in the journey every business runs, again and again. Each stage has problems; every problem becomes content.

Find customers → Qualify → Build relationships → Close → Deliver
      → Retain → Grow → Learn → (repeat)

This map is how we organise everything else: a post, an article, or a customer story can always be placed at a stage — and a stage always maps to one of the five commercial questions.

5. Commercial Problems — by stage

We spend our time here, because problems are what customers actually feel. Think business, not software.

Finding customers — wrong customers · expensive leads · poor referrals · weak marketing · no differentiation.

Qualifying customers — everyone treated the same · reps chase every lead · time wasted · poor discovery · wrong opportunities.

Building relationships — slow follow-up · inconsistent communication · lost trust · no customer history · staff leave and relationships disappear with them.

Closing sales — deals stall and nobody knows why · discounting · decision-makers disappear · competitors arrive late and win.

Retention — customers quietly leave · nobody notices · complaints ignored · no engagement · no renewal process.

Growth — no upselling · existing customers forgotten · no cross-selling · no segmentation.

Each bullet is a post. Each cluster is an article. Each stage is a campaign.

6. Commercial Questions

Possibly our strongest content, because people search LinkedIn and Google with questions, not features. Answer one a week.

  • Why do businesses lose customers?
  • Why do customers stop replying?
  • Why do good salespeople fail?
  • Why are forecasts wrong?
  • Why do customers choose competitors?
  • How do businesses actually grow?
  • Why do businesses buy CRM and never use it?
  • Why does follow-up fail?
  • Why do referrals dry up?
  • Why do sales meetings waste time?

7. Myths vs Reality

People love a myth, well corrected. It’s memorable and it positions us as the ones who see clearly.

Myth Reality
CRM improves sales. CRM only improves sales when people use it consistently.
More leads create more revenue. Better conversion often creates more revenue.
Customers leave because of price. Customers often leave because they feel forgotten.
AI replaces salespeople. AI helps salespeople make better decisions.

8. Jobs To Be Done

Forget software. What job is the customer hiring us to do? Say it in their words.

  • “I need to know who to call.”
  • “I need to know which deals matter.”
  • “I need better forecasts.”
  • “I need my team to follow one sales process.”
  • “I need to stop losing customers.”
  • “I need to grow existing accounts.”
  • “I need visibility.”

9. Outcomes

Customers don’t buy CRM. They buy the result. Lead with the outcome, always.

More sales · better relationships · predictable growth · less wasted effort · more confidence · faster follow-up · happier customers · better decisions.

10. Vocabulary — the words we use, and the ones we avoid

Almost nobody documents this, and it’s why most B2B messaging sounds the same. Until the market understands what we do, we speak the customer’s language, not the industry’s.

Use (the customer’s words) Avoid (until we’ve earned them)
Customers · Leads · Sales · Growth Commercial intelligence · Digital transformation
Relationships · Follow-up · Revenue AI-powered ecosystem · Revenue operating system
Opportunities · Business · Decisions Customer orchestration · Data democratization · Next-generation platform

“Commercial Health” is the one elevated term we deliberately teach — because we define it, own it, and make it concrete.

11. Signature Ideas — our intellectual property

A handful of sharp lines we repeat until people quote them back to us. These become ours.

  • Every business is a commercial system.
  • Customer relationships are measurable.
  • Follow-up is a competitive advantage.
  • Every lost customer leaves evidence.
  • Revenue is the outcome of better decisions.
  • Clean customer data creates better business decisions.
  • ⭐ Manage customers with evidence, not memory.

The last one is our flagship — it fuses our two beliefs (evidence over opinion; relationships as an asset) into six words, and it points straight at Commercial Health.

Part C — Putting it to work

12. From framework to content

This is the recipe that turns the toolkit into a repeatable content machine — and the direct bridge to the Content Strategy’s pillars. To build any piece:

  1. Pick a stage of the commercial journey (Section 4) → this sets the topic.
  2. Name the problem the customer feels there (Section 5) → this earns attention.
  3. Open with a belief or a myth (Sections 3, 7) → this sets our point of view.
  4. Frame it as a question or a job (Sections 6, 8) → this matches how they search and think.
  5. Land on the outcome (Section 9) → this is what they actually want.
  6. Close with a signature idea (Section 11) → this is what they remember.
  7. Tie it to Commercial Health → this is what makes it ours, not generic CRM advice.

Worked example. Stage: Retention. Problem: customers quietly leave. Myth: “customers leave over price.” Reality: they leave because they feel forgotten. Job: “I need to stop losing customers.” Outcome: happier customers who stay. Signature idea: every lost customer leaves evidence. Commercial Health tie-in: retention is a vital sign — and it’s measurable.

13. Messaging assets — how we describe ourselves

Consistent descriptions, ready to paste into a bio, a deck, or a first conversation.

  • One-liner: Sigmoid CRM helps businesses improve their Commercial Health — so they win more customers, keep them longer, and grow them.
  • Short (a sentence more): Most businesses manage customer relationships from memory. Sigmoid CRM helps them manage with evidence — making their Commercial Health visible so they can find, win, keep, and grow customers with confidence.
  • Medium (for an About section): Sigmoid CRM is an analytics-native CRM built on one belief: revenue is the outcome of better commercial decisions. We help businesses see their Commercial Health — lead quality, conversion, follow-up, retention, growth — and improve it, using clean data and evidence rather than opinion and memory. Part of Sigmoid Analytica: we make the world transparent.

14. Message discipline — the rules of consistency

Messaging works through repetition and restraint. The framework only pays off if we hold the line.

  • One big idea. Everything ladders to Commercial Health. If it doesn’t, it doesn’t ship.
  • Repeat on purpose. The signature ideas and vocabulary are said again and again, not reinvented.
  • Problem before product. We talk about the customer’s commercial problem far more than our features.
  • Plain words. We use the customer’s vocabulary; we avoid the jargon list until we’ve earned it.
  • Evidence, not hype. We say what we can support and how sure we are — the honesty is the brand.
  • Outcome first. We lead with what the customer gets, not what the software has.

In one line

Sigmoid is the company that helps businesses manage customers with evidence, not memory — and measure and improve their Commercial Health. Say that, in a hundred ways, until the market says it back.
