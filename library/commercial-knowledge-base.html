The Sigmoid Commercial Knowledge Base

The taxonomy of commercial concepts — the syllabus for everything we teach

Version: 0.1 (Draft) · Status: Working document · Owner: [Name] · Last updated: [Date] Inherits from: The Sigmoid Commercial System · feeds The Sigmoid Editorial System.

What this is

Every idea we teach is a concept. This document is the organised list of them — the syllabus behind all our content, courses, and product explanations.

Two rules make it powerful:

  1. Every concept is a content atom. One concept becomes an article, a LinkedIn post, a carousel, a webinar, a customer guide, a course lesson, a book chapter, and a feature explanation.
  2. Every concept has a home in the system. Concepts are grouped by the Sigmoid Commercial System — the five commercial questions and the commercial journey — so nothing is off-topic and every piece of content can name its lineage (see The Sigmoid Editorial System, §2).

Concepts are grouped A–H and numbered, so each has a stable id (e.g. B-07 Lead Quality) the editorial system can track. This is a living taxonomy: the list below is the comprehensive core (~180 concepts) and is designed to grow toward the full 300–500 as we teach.

How each concept becomes content

Every entry above is a reusable atom. The editorial system draws from it and expands it into formats:

From one concept… …we can produce
e.g. B-07 Lead Quality a LinkedIn post · a carousel · an article · a webinar · a customer guide · a course lesson · a chapter · a product-feature explanation

That is the leverage: write the concept once, teach it many ways. A single well-understood concept can fuel a month of content across channels — and the same concept explains a product capability to a buyer.

Using and growing the taxonomy

  • Tag every piece of content with its concept id(s). Over time this shows what we’ve taught, what we’ve over-covered, and the gaps.
  • Add concepts as the market teaches us — new objections, new questions, new language captured as evidence become new entries. The Knowledge Base grows the way the SCS says everything should: from evidence.
  • The Academy modules (Commercial Health, Decisions, Learning, Systems, Intelligence, AI, Analytics) are simply clusters of these concepts, sequenced.
