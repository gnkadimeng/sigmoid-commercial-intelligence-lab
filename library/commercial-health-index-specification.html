Commercial Health Index — Specification

One specification, many implementations — the canonical definition of Commercial Health

Version: 0.1 (Draft) · Status: Product Specification · Authors: Sigmoid Analytica Inherits from: The Sigmoid Commercial System, Part IV · aligned with the Commercial Intelligence Specification (CIS).

This is the canonical definition of the Commercial Health Index (CHI). Every place a Commercial Health score appears — the website assessment, the CRM dashboard, onboarding, consulting, executive reports — MUST compute it from this specification. One definition; many surfaces. Where a surface and this document disagree, this document governs.

1. Introduction

The Sigmoid Commercial System argues that Commercial Health is observable and can be measured. This specification makes that operational: it defines what the Commercial Health Index measures, how it is scored, how confident we are in it, how it is interpreted, and what to do to improve it — in a way that is independent of any single implementation.

The CHI turns a promise (“score your Commercial Health”) into a product. It also changes the sales conversation from “here is our CRM” to “your Commercial Health score is 48/100 — here is why, here is what to improve first, and here is how the platform helps.” That is consultative, and it is only possible if the score means the same thing everywhere. This document guarantees that.

2. Scope & Principles

The CHI Specification governs the measurement of Commercial Health for an organisation. It defines the model, scoring, evidence, confidence, interpretation, and improvement logic. It does not prescribe a user interface; it prescribes the numbers and their meaning.

Governing principles (inherited from the SCS and CIS):

  1. Transparent — no black box. Every score can be explained down to its inputs.
  2. Evidence-graded — a score always carries how it was derived and how confident we are.
  3. Observable — every dimension reduces to indicators that can be seen or measured.
  4. Improvable — every score points to a next action, not just a grade.
  5. Weights are data — weightings are declared, versioned, and tunable, never hidden.
  6. Opinion → evidence — the CHI is designed to graduate from self-report to measurement.

3. The Commercial Health Model

Commercial Health is composed of seven dimensions (the canonical set from SCS Part IV). Each is scored 0–100; the CHI is their weighted mean.

# Dimension Definition
D1 Lead Quality The degree to which the organisation attracts the right customers, from sources it understands.
D2 Sales Effectiveness The degree to which the right opportunities are converted, through a repeatable process.
D3 Relationship Quality The strength, currency, and continuity of customer relationships.
D4 Customer Retention The organisation’s ability to keep customers and detect risk early.
D5 Customer Growth The degree to which existing relationships grow in value over time.
D6 Revenue Predictability The reliability of forecasts and the honesty of the pipeline.
D7 Decision Quality The degree to which commercial decisions are made from evidence and reviewed.

4. Indicators

Each dimension is expressed through indicators. There are two kinds, and the CHI supports both:

  • Self-assessed indicators — statements a person rates (the basis of the website assessment).
  • Measured signals — quantities computed from data (the basis of the CRM dashboard).

The same dimension can be scored either way; the difference is confidence (Section 8).

Dim Self-assessed indicators (rate 1–5) Measured signals (from data)
D1 Clear ICP and most leads match it · we know which sources produce customers who stay · we de-prioritise poor-fit leads lead→customer rate by source · % leads matching ICP · cost per won customer
D2 One consistent sales process · we know stage-by-stage conversion · we win most opportunities we pursue stage conversion rates · overall win rate · sales-cycle length
D3 History available to the whole team · relationships survive staff leaving · we follow up when we say follow-up completion rate · time-to-follow-up · % contacts with recent activity
D4 We spot at-risk customers early · we have a renewal process · we know why customers leave churn rate · early-warning coverage · renewal rate
D5 We actively grow accounts · we segment by value · existing customers drive real revenue expansion revenue % · net revenue retention · share of revenue from existing customers
D6 Forecasts are usually close · pipeline reflects reality · leaders see performance without asking forecast vs actual variance · pipeline coverage ratio · stale-deal %
D7 Decisions made from evidence not memory · we can explain why we pursued/dropped deals · we review past decisions % decisions with recorded rationale · decision-review cadence · evidence coverage

5. Assessment Questions

The self-assessment instrument is the canonical question set (used directly by the website assessment and onboarding). Each dimension has three statements, rated 1 (strongly disagree) to 5 (strongly agree). The 21 statements are those listed as self-assessed indicators in Section 4. Implementations MUST use these exact statements so scores are comparable across surfaces.

6. Scoring Model

The scoring is transparent and deterministic.

  • Indicator score. A self-assessed answer a ∈ {1..5} → (a − 1) / 4 × 100 (a value 0–100). A measured signal is mapped to 0–100 by its declared rubric/benchmark (Section 13).
  • Dimension score Dᵢ = the mean of that dimension’s indicator scores.
  • Commercial Health Index CHI = the weighted mean of the seven dimension scores using the weights in Section 7.
  • All scores are reported on 0–100, rounded to the nearest whole number, alongside their confidence (Section 8).
indicator (0–100) → dimension = mean(indicators) → CHI = Σ(wᵢ · Dᵢ)

7. Weighting Model

Weights are declared data, not hidden logic. At v0.1 the seven dimensions are equally weighted (1/7 each). This is a starting hypothesis; weights MUST be versioned and MAY be tuned as evidence accumulates about which dimensions most predict commercial outcomes.

Dim D1 D2 D3 D4 D5 D6 D7
Weight (v0.1) 0.143 0.143 0.143 0.143 0.143 0.143 0.143

8. Evidence & Confidence Model

Borrowing directly from the CIS: a CHI score is never just a number — it carries a confidence grade that reflects the evidence behind it.

Evidence behind a score Confidence grade
Self-report only (assessment) C — Indicative
A single measured signal B — Substantiated
Converged, benchmarked measured signals A — Validated
No usable input D — Hypothesis

Each dimension reports its own confidence, and the overall CHI reports the lowest confidence among the dimensions that materially drive it. A score is always shown as value + grade — e.g., “CHI 48 (grade C, self-reported).” This is what keeps the index honest and consultative: we never imply more certainty than the evidence supports.

9. Interpretation Model

The CHI maps onto the SCS commercial-maturity ladder, so a score is a diagnosis and a next step — not a grade to feel good or bad about.

CHI Band Meaning
0–20 1 · Reactive Commercial decisions are ad hoc, from memory
21–40 2 · Organised Process exists; decisions rest on opinion
41–60 3 · Measured Performance is tracked; metrics inform decisions
61–80 4 · Evidence-Driven Decisions made from evidence, with confidence
81–100 5 · Commercial Intelligence The business improves by design

Interpretation MUST always surface the lowest-scoring dimensions as the priority. The index is a map of where to improve.

10. Improvement Model

For every dimension, the specification defines the next move that raises it — and names how the Sigmoid platform helps. This is what turns a score into a conversation.

Dim If it’s low, the next move… …and the platform helps by
D1 Lead Quality Define the ICP; track source → outcome Capturing lead source and linking it to won/lost
D2 Sales Effectiveness Adopt one process; measure stage conversion Enforcing stages and computing conversion
D3 Relationship Quality Make follow-up a system, not a memory Follow-up tasks, cadences, shared history
D4 Customer Retention Build early-warning + a renewal process Engagement signals and at-risk flags
D5 Customer Growth Segment by value; plan expansion Account segmentation and whitespace views
D6 Revenue Predictability Clean the pipeline; compare forecast to actual Pipeline hygiene and forecast-vs-actual
D7 Decision Quality Record decision rationale; review it Evidence capture and decision provenance

11. Implementations

One specification, many implementations. Each surface computes the same CHI from this document.

Implementation Mode Confidence Purpose
Website assessment Self-assessed (21 questions) C Lead magnet; instant score + top gaps + book a walkthrough
CRM dashboard Measured from CRM data B → A over time Live Commercial Health, tracked like financial health
Onboarding Self-assessed baseline C Sets the customer’s improvement priorities from day one
Consulting engagement Self + measured + interviews up to A Full diagnosis and an improvement roadmap
Executive report Measured, over time B/A CHI trend beside financial health, for leadership

The sales motion the CHI enables: “Your Commercial Health is 48 (grade C). Your weakest dimensions are Retention and Revenue Predictability. Here’s why, here’s what to fix first, and here’s exactly how the platform moves those two.”

12. Data Model

For measured mode, each dimension declares its required inputs (no hidden variables, per the CIS). Where inputs are unavailable, the dimension falls back to self-assessed mode and its confidence drops to C. Implementations MUST record which mode produced each dimension score.

13. Governance, Versioning & Validation

  • The CHI model, indicators, questions, and weights are versioned; changes are made deliberately and recorded (append-only, like the CIS).
  • Weight and rubric changes require a rationale and, ideally, evidence.
  • The scoring logic MUST be covered by automated tests (ranges 0–100, missing-value safety, determinism) — the same testing standard as every Sigmoid framework.
  • Validation follows the CIS validation model: logical (the maths runs), statistical (do the dimensions predict outcomes?), and continuous (has new evidence changed the weights?).

14. Relationship to the SCS and CIS

  • SCS Part IV defines what Commercial Health is. This spec defines how it is measured.
  • The CIS governs how evidence and confidence work; the CHI adopts its evidence and confidence model wholesale.
  • The Commercial Intelligence Lab is the natural home of the reference implementation: the seven dimensions and weights as a framework YAML, the scoring as a tested function, the assessment as an interactive page.

15. Success Criteria

A CHI implementation compliant with this specification shall:

  1. Produce the same score from the same inputs, on any surface.
  2. Always report a score with its confidence grade.
  3. Explain any score down to its indicators.
  4. Surface the lowest dimensions as the improvement priority.
  5. Support both self-assessed and measured modes, and record which was used.
  6. Improve over time as inputs move from opinion to evidence.

16. Future Work

  • Empirically tune the dimension weights against real commercial outcomes.
  • Define benchmark rubrics for each measured signal (industry / segment).
  • Add automated confidence computation from data coverage.
  • Longitudinal CHI (trend, velocity of improvement) as a leading indicator in its own right.

Commercial Health is the organising idea behind everything Sigmoid does. Competitors can copy CRM features, dashboards, even AI. They cannot easily copy a rigorously defined, consistently implemented, evidence-graded measure of a business’s ability to grow. This specification is how we make that measure real — and keep it ours.
