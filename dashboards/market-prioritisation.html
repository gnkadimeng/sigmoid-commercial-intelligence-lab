Interpretation

  • A high score means a segment scores well on the weighted blend of size, pain, budget, and fit, after penalising competition and long sales cycles.
  • evidence_quality_score deliberately discounts segments we know little about — a hedge against ranking on guesswork.
  • The lead over #2 is a scan of robustness: a small margin means the top choice is sensitive to weight changes and should not be over-read.
WarningLimitations
  • Sub-scores are assumptions encoded as numbers, not observations; the ranking inherits their bias.
  • Synthetic data only — nothing here is a decision. Promoting a segment to a recommendation requires evidence collection and validation (see Working Paper Ch. 5).
NoteNext step in the lifecycle

Collect real evidence for the top-ranked segments (interviews, pilots), register it in the evidence register, and re-run. The ranking should change as evidence — not assumptions — accumulates.

## Interpretation

- A high score means a segment scores well on the **weighted** blend of size, pain, budget, and fit,
  after **penalising** competition and long sales cycles.
- `evidence_quality_score` deliberately discounts segments we know little about — a hedge against
  ranking on guesswork.
- The lead over #2 is a scan of **robustness**: a small margin means the top choice is sensitive to
  weight changes and should not be over-read.

::: {.callout-warning appearance="simple"}
## Limitations
- Sub-scores are **assumptions encoded as numbers**, not observations; the ranking inherits their
  bias.
- Synthetic data only — nothing here is a decision. Promoting a segment to a recommendation requires
  evidence collection and validation (see [Working Paper Ch. 5](../working-paper/05-market-prioritisation.qmd)).
:::
